### a)

$\text{Por Ley de Coloumb: }F_{12}=K_{e}\frac{q_{1}q_{2}}{r^2}\hat{r}_{12}$
$\implies \cancel{K_{e}}\left( \frac{Qq}{\cancel{a^2}}\hat{j}+\frac{Qq}{\cancel{a^2}}\hat{i}+\frac{Q^2}{2\cancel{a^2}}\left( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2};\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \right)\right)=0$

$\text{Para x (analogo a y)}:\cancel{ Q }q+\frac{\sqrt{ 2 }}{4}Q^\cancel{ 2 }=0$
$$\therefore  q=-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}Q$$
### b)
Asumamos que si: $\implies \sum F_{q_{1}}=0$
$\implies \cancel{K_{e}}\left(- \frac{Qq}{\cancel{a^2}}\hat{j}-\frac{Qq}{\cancel{a^2}}\hat{i}+\frac{q^2}{2\cancel{a^2}}\left( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2};-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \right)\right)=0$
$\implies -Qq \hat{j}-Qq \hat{i}+q^2\left( \frac{\sqrt{ 2 }}{2};-\frac{\sqrt{ 2 }}{2} \right)=0$

$\text{Para y}:-Q\cancel{q}-\frac{\sqrt{ 2 }}{4}q^{\cancel2}=0$
Utilizando la formula del primer inciso: $q=-\frac{\sqrt{ 2 }}{4}Q$
$$\begin{align}
-Q-\frac{\sqrt{ 2 }}{4}\left( -\frac{\sqrt{ 2}}{4}Q \right)=0 \\
Q-\frac{2}{16}Q=0
\end{align}$$

Asumiendo que $Q\neq 0$

$$\implies 1-\frac{2}{16}=0 \to \text{Abs!}\qquad\therefore \text{No es posible}$$
