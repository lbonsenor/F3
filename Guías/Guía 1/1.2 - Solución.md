Datos: 
- $q=+4\mu C$
- $x_{1}=0\text{m}$
- $x_{2}=8\text{m}$
- $K_{e}=9\cdot 10^9 \frac{\text{Nm}^2}{\text{c}^2}$

Sabemos que, en este caso: $$\vec{E}=\frac{K_{e}}{r_{1}^2}q\cdot\hat{r_{1}}+\frac{K_{e}}{r_{2}^2}q\cdot \hat{r_{2}}$$
### a)
$\text{Para x=10m}: \vec{E}=K_{e}q\left( \frac{1}{100\text{m}^2}\hat{i}+\frac{1}{4\text{m}^2}\hat{i} \right)=9360\frac{\text{N}}{\text{C}}\hat{i}$
$\text{Para x=2m}:\vec{E}=K_{e}q\left( \frac{1}{4\text{m}^2}\hat{i}-\frac{1}{36\text{m}^2}\hat{i} \right)=8000\frac{\text{N}}{\text{C}}\hat{i}$

### b)
Ya de por sí sabemos de que si $x=\pm \infty \implies \vec{E}=0$
$\vec{E}=\frac{\cancel{K_{e}}}{r_{1}^2}\cancel{q}\cdot\hat{r_{1}}+\frac{\cancel{K_{e}}}{r_{2}^2}\cancel{q}\cdot \hat{r_{2}}=0$
	$\frac{1}{r_{1}^2}\hat{r_{1}}+\frac{1}{r_{2}^2}\hat{r_{2}}=0$
	$\frac{1}{x^2}\hat{r_{1}}+\frac{1}{(x-8\text{m})^2}=0$
	$\frac{1}{x^2}\hat{r_{1}}=-\frac{1}{x^2-16x\text{m}+64\text{m}^2}\hat{r_{2}}$

Caso 1: $\hat{r_{1}}=-\hat{r_{2}}$
	$x^2=x^2-16x\text{m}+64\text{m}^2 \to x=4\text{m}$

Caso 2: $\hat{r_{1}}=\hat{r_{2}}$ 
	$x^2=-x^{2}+16x\text{m}+64\text{m}^2\to \nexists x$

$$\therefore x=4\text{m}\quad;\quad x=\pm \infty$$
